### 1. Main Lift — Trap Bar Deadlift

**First session with the trap bar.**

The trap bar shifts load slightly more toward the quads and reduces lower back moment arm compared to conventional.

It is more sport-specific for triathlon — the upright torso mirrors cycling position and the loading pattern transfers directly to running.

Expect to lift more than conventional at the same RPE.

Your conventional deadlift top set was 4×180 lb.

Estimated trap bar starting point: **195–205 lb working**, but we ramp up to find it today.

*Left glute activation before first working set: 10 slow single-leg glute bridges on left, 2s hold at top.*

| Set | Reps | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | 5 | 95 lb | Feel the new position |
| Warm-up | 5 | 135 lb | Neutral spine, drive through floor |
| Working | 5 | 165 lb | |
| Working | 5 | 185 lb | |
| Working | 4 | 200 lb | Estimate — adjust based on feel |
| Top set | 4 | 205–215 lb | RPE 8 target |

*Rest: 90s between working sets.*

*Key cues: stand inside the trap bar, grip the handles, push the floor away rather than pulling the bar up. Keep chest tall throughout.*

*Report back: top set weight and RPE — this becomes your Race Prep working weight.*

## Progression notes

Trap bar deadlift: whatever top set you hit today is the Race Prep anchor.

Back squat: if 160–165 lb felt RPE ≤ 8, that's the new working weight.

Hip thrust: if 235 lb felt RPE ≤ 8, move to 235 lb working next legs session.

Lateral lunge: first session — note how hip and adductor felt, any tightness.

Alternate back squat and hip thrust as the secondary lift each week — squat this week, hip thrust next week.
