## PT Warm-up — Psoas Activation
**Do before any loading · ~8 min**

| Exercise | Sets | Load |
|---|---|---|
| Psoas arm raises | 2 | Bodyweight |
| Psoas lean back | 2 | Bodyweight |
| Psoas hold — left | 2 | Hold per PT protocol |
| Psoas hold — right | 2 | Hold per PT protocol |
| Side-lying leg arc — left | 2 | 10 reps over dumbbell |
| Side-lying leg arc — right | 2 | 10 reps over dumbbell |

**Side-lying leg arc setup:** Lie on your side, dumbbell placed on the floor in front of you. Arc the top leg up, over the dumbbell, and behind — then reverse back to start. 10 reps each direction counts as one set.

*Activates glute med and external rotators — important for knee tracking under squat load and IT band health.*

*Psoas activation before squatting improves hip position and reduces anterior pelvic tilt under load.*

## 1. Main Lift — Barbell Back Squat
**5 sets · 3–4 min rest between sets**

**Note:** No prior barbell back squat data. Use this session to establish your working weight. Follow the ramp and stop adding load when the set feels like RPE 8. Record your top set weight — that becomes your Week 2 starting point.

| Set | Reps | Weight |
|-----|------|--------|
| 1 (warm-up) | 8 | 45 lb (bar only) |
| 2 | 5 | 75 lb |
| 3 | 5 | 95 lb |
| 4 | 4 | 115 lb |
| 5 | 4 | Add load only if set 4 felt RPE 7 or below |

**Cues:** Bar on traps, chest tall, knees track over toes, hip crease below parallel, 3 sec descent.

**RPE target:** 8 on top sets.

*Post-bike fatigue means these will feel harder than they would fresh. Factor that in — don't chase a number today, chase the RPE.*

## 2. Secondary — Hip Thrust (Barbell)
**4 sets · 2 min rest between sets**

| Set | Reps | Weight |
|-----|------|--------|
| 1 (warm-up) | 8 | 135 lb |
| 2 | 4 | 205 lb |
| 3 | 4 | 215 lb |
| 4 | 4 | 225 lb |

**Cues:** Shoulders on bench, bar padded across hips, drive through heels, full hip extension at top, 1 sec hold. Chin tucked — don't hyperextend the neck.

**RPE target:** 8 on sets 2–4.

*Your strongest movement. Dropping to 4 reps to shift into strength range — load stays at your PR ceiling.*

---

## 3. Accessory A — Alternating Lunge (Dumbbell)
**4 sets · 90 sec rest between sets**

| Set | Reps | Weight (per hand) |
|-----|------|-------------------|
| 1 | 8 each | 25 lb |
| 2 | 6 each | 30 lb |
| 3 | 6 each | 30 lb |
| 4 | 6 each | 30 lb |

**Cues:** Step long enough that front shin stays vertical, back knee just above floor, drive through front heel to return.

*Your PR is 30 lb per hand — maintaining that load, dropping to 6 reps per side for strength range.*

---

## 4. Accessory B — Step-Up (Dumbbell)
**3 sets each side · 90 sec rest between sets**

| Set | Reps | Weight (per hand) |
|-----|------|-------------------|
| 1 | 5 | 20 lb |
| 2 | 5 | 25 lb |
| 3 | 5 | 25 lb |

**Setup:** Use a sturdy box or step at roughly knee height.

**Cues:** Drive through the heel of the elevated foot — don't push off the back foot. Full hip extension at top before stepping down.

*New movement — starting conservatively. This is highly specific to running and cycling power. We'll progress load quickly once pattern is clean.*

## Post-Session Notes to Record
After this session write down:

## Progression Rules (Week 2+)
- Back squat: once working weight is established this week, add 5–10 lb to top sets when RPE is 7 or below
- Hip thrust: add 5–10 lb when all working sets complete at RPE 7 or below
- Lunge: add 2–5 lb per hand when 4 × 6 feels RPE 7 or below
- Step-up: add 5 lb per hand when all sets complete at RPE 7 or below

---

> **Shin rule:** Any anterior shin pain or point tenderness during step-ups = stop immediately, drop that movement for the day, flag before next run session.
