## Context — Progression from Last Week

Last Tuesday: 2×5min at 7:15/mi, HR 157–166, legs felt good, clean mechanics.

This week: **3×5min at the same effort.** One more interval, same pace and HR target.

The additional interval is the training stimulus — your body has to sustain threshold-adjacent effort for longer total time.

Expect interval 3 to feel meaningfully harder than intervals 1 and 2.

That difficulty is the point.

---

## The Run

**Total structure: ~40 minutes**

**Warm-up — 10 minutes easy**

HR ceiling 148 bpm.

Genuinely easy — protects interval quality.

---

**Interval 1 — 5 minutes**

Target HR: 150–160 bpm.

Target pace: ~7:15–7:30/mi.

Go by HR, not pace.

---

**Recovery — 3 minutes easy jog**

HR back below 148 bpm.

Keep moving — do not walk.

---

**Interval 2 — 5 minutes**

Same target: HR 150–160 bpm.

Expect this to feel harder than interval 1.

If HR climbs above 163: back off pace slightly.

---

**Recovery — 3 minutes easy jog**

HR back toward 145–148 bpm.

---

**Interval 3 — 5 minutes**

Same target: HR 150–160 bpm.

This will be the hardest interval.

Maintain form over pace if it starts to slip — cadence at 170+ spm, don't shuffle.

If HR climbs above 165: reduce pace to hold form rather than pushing through.

---

**Easy running — 8 minutes**

HR back to easy aerobic range.

Shake out the legs.

No strides today — three intervals is enough quality work.

## Contrast therapy

Post-run as usual.

## Auto-regulation

If achilles tightens during warm-up: drop to 2×5min intervals only.

If achilles tightens during interval 2: finish it, skip interval 3, easy jog home.

The three intervals are the goal — the achilles is the constraint.

How interval 3 felt compared to intervals 1 and 2.
