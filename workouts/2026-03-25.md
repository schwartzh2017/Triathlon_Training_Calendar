## The Run

**Duration:** 30min continuous

**Effort:** Genuinely easy.

Not "easy but picking it up on the hills."

Not "easy but keeping pace with someone."

Truly easy — conversational, controlled, deliberate.

**HR ceiling: 155 bpm.**

If HR climbs above 155: slow to a walk until it drops back to 145, then resume running.

No exceptions.

**Pace guidance:** Based on Wednesday's run last week (8→9→10 min/mile fade with HR to 172), your true aerobic pace right now is probably 10:00–11:00/mi.

That will feel embarrassingly slow.

Run it anyway.

This is not a fitness test.

This is bone loading and cardiovascular adaptation stimulus.

**At the 15-minute mark:** Note any shin sensation consciously.

If anything feels off from baseline: finish the run at walk pace.

---

## What success looks like

Completing 30 minutes with HR staying mostly under 155 and legs feeling easy.

That's it.

Pace is irrelevant.

Effort is irrelevant.

Consistency of aerobic stimulus is what builds the cardiorespiratory system right now.

---

## Post-run check

Note your max HR, avg HR, and how your shins feel immediately after and 2 hours after.

Report both numbers in your next workout log.

---

## Progression trigger for Week 4

If this run stays under 155 avg HR and feels controlled: we add 5 minutes next week (35min) and introduce one very short stride block (4×15s strides at 5k effort) at the end.

If HR is still elevated and fading: we hold at 30min easy for one more week.
