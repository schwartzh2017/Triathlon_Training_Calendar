## This is a milestone session

This is your first continuous 30min run since your tibial stress fracture.

The goal today is not pace, distance, or effort.

The goal is completion — 30 minutes of continuous running with zero anterior shin pain.

Everything else is secondary.

---

## Non-negotiables before you start
- Eat at least 20–30g carbohydrate within 60 min prior

- No fasted running — ever, during this cycle

- Check your Whoop recovery score before heading out. Below 33% = postpone to tomorrow.

---

## Session Structure

### Warm-up — 5 min
Easy walk.

Gradually increase pace.

Do not start running until you feel fully loose.

### Run — 30 min continuous
**Pace:** Conversational. You should be able to speak in full sentences throughout.

**HR target:** Z2 — below 140 bpm if possible. Do not let HR creep into Z3 today.

**Effort:** RPE 4–5 out of 10. This should feel almost too easy. That is correct.

*If it feels too slow, you are doing it right.*

### Cool-down — 5 min
Easy walk.

Do not stop abruptly — walk until HR is below 100.

---

## Shin monitoring — mandatory checkpoints

Check in with your shins at each of these points:

**At 10 min:** Any anterior shin sensation? Aching, tightness, point tenderness?

- None → continue

- Mild aching that fades within 1–2 min → note it, continue cautiously, recheck at 15 min

- Point tenderness or sharp sensation → stop immediately, walk home

**At 20 min:** Same check.

- None → continue to 30 min

- Any new sensation → stop, walk home

**At 30 min finish:** Note any shin feedback, even minor.

*Any point tenderness at any point = walk home, do not run through it, contact your medical team before the next run session.*

---

## After the run
Within 30 min:
- 25–40g protein

- Note how your shins feel immediately post-run and again 2–4 hours later

- Note it in your Whoop journal — shin status, overall RPE, how legs felt

Post-run shin aching that resolves within 1 hour is normal adaptation.

Post-run shin aching that persists beyond 2 hours = flag to your medical team.
