---

## Non-negotiables before you start

Collagen + tart cherry juice 60min before heading out — set it up the night before if needed.

**5 minute walk before running — non-negotiable.**

Achilles are not warm at 6am.

Do not start running cold.

Walk out of the house, walk the first 5 minutes, then transition to easy running.

Shin and achilles check mid-walk.

---

## The Run

**This is your quality run session of the week.**

Thursday = strides and run fitness building.

Saturday = brick run (transition adaptation).

Sunday = longer easy run (aerobic base).

Three different purposes, three different sessions.

---

**First 20 minutes — easy aerobic**

**HR ceiling: 150 bpm.**

Start slow — morning HR is lower and tissue is cold.

9:30–10:30/mi expected.

Genuinely easy for the full 20 minutes.

This protects the quality of the strides at the end.

---

**Minutes 20–25 — 4 strides**

Begin strides once you've completed 20 minutes of easy running.

Each stride:

- Gradual acceleration over 20 seconds to approximately 85–90% effort
- Smooth and controlled — not a sprint
- Decelerate naturally over 5–10 seconds
- Walk 40 seconds before the next stride

| Stride | Duration | Effort | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 20s | 80% — ease in | 40s walk |
| 2 | 20s | 85% | 40s walk |
| 3 | 20s | 85% | 40s walk |
| 4 | 20s | 85% | Done |

*If achilles feels tight at any point during strides: stop strides, walk home.*

*Strides should feel like controlled speed, not pain.*

---

## Achilles protocol — mandatory post-run

Before going inside:

**Straight-leg calf stretch on step:** 30s each side × 2 rounds.

**Bent-knee calf stretch on step:** 30s each side × 2 rounds.

Total: ~4 minutes.

Then go get ready for work.

---

## What to report back

Achilles status — before, during, after strides.

Any swelling.

HR avg and max.

How the strides felt compared to Sunday's strides.

How morning running feels vs evening — note the difference for future scheduling.
