## What is a Brick Session?

A brick is a bike-to-run workout done back-to-back with minimal transition.

The name comes from how your legs feel when you start running off the bike — like bricks.

The first 5–10 minutes of the run will feel awful.

Your legs will feel heavy, coordination will feel off, and your HR will spike.

**This is completely normal.** It's the neuromuscular system switching from cycling-specific muscle recruitment to running-specific patterns.

The goal of brick training is to practice this transition so your body adapts and the "brick legs" feeling shortens over time.

By race day you want the first kilometer of the run to feel manageable, not like a disaster.

**Today is your first exposure.** The only goal is to experience it and keep moving.

---

## Bike — Moderate Aerobic Effort

**This is not a threshold session.**

Your legs need enough stimulus to create the bike-to-run transition feeling, but not so much fatigue that the run is impossible.

**Duration:** 50–60min

**Target effort:** Aerobic endurance — HR 138–155 bpm sustained

**IF target:** 0.75–0.82 — sweet spot, not threshold

**Cadence:** 85–90 rpm

Good SYSTM options:
- Any sweet spot or tempo workout 50–60min
- An outdoor moderate ride if weather allows

**Do not pick a threshold workout today.** Save threshold for Tuesday.

---

## Transition — T2 Practice

The moment you finish the bike:

Rack or lean your bike.

Change shoes if needed.

Start running within 60–90 seconds of finishing the bike.

No sitting down. No stretching. No waiting.

The faster you transition, the more you train the specific adaptation.

---

## Run — Off the Bike

**Duration:** 15–20min

**HR ceiling:** 158 bpm

**Expect the first 5 minutes to feel terrible.** This is normal. Keep moving.

The legs will feel heavy and uncoordinated. Your HR will likely spike 10–15 bpm higher than normal for the same perceived effort.

**Pace:** Ignore it completely. Run by feel and HR only.

If HR spikes above 158: slow to a shuffle — don't walk unless you have to, keep the running pattern going.

By minutes 8–10 your legs should start feeling more normal.

**Achilles protocol:** Check achilles immediately after finishing the run. Note any tightness or swelling.

Post-run mandatory calf stretch — straight-leg and bent-knee, 30s each side × 2 rounds.

## What success looks like today

You completed the transition and ran for 15–20 minutes after the bike.

That's it.

Pace, HR, feel — all secondary.

The data point I want is: at what minute did your legs start feeling normal?
