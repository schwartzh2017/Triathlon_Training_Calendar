## 1. Main Lift — Narrow Grip Bench Press (Barbell)
**4 sets · 2 min rest between sets**

Your PR is 70 lb. Dropping to strength rep range.

| Set | Reps | Weight |
|-----|------|--------|
| 1 (warm-up) | 8 | 55 lb |
| 2 | 5 | 70 lb |
| 3 | 5 | 70 lb |
| 4 | 5 | 75 lb |

**Cues:** Narrow grip (hands just inside shoulder width), elbows tracking at ~45° — not flared wide, controlled 3 sec descent, full lockout at top.

**RPE target:** 8 on sets 2–4.

*Narrow grip protects the shoulder joint under load and recruits triceps more directly — better for triathlon than wide grip.*

*If set 4 at 75 lb feels RPE 9+, hold 70 lb across all working sets next week.*

## 2. Accessory A — Arnold Press, Seated (Dumbbell)
**4 sets · 90 sec rest between sets**

Your PR is 20 lb per hand. Maintaining load, dropping to strength rep range.

| Set | Reps | Weight (per hand) |
|-----|------|-------------------|
| 1 | 5 | 18 lb |
| 2 | 5 | 20 lb |
| 3 | 5 | 20 lb |
| 4 | 4 | 20 lb |

**Cues:** Start with palms facing you, rotate to palms forward as you press overhead. Full range — don't cut the rotation short.

**RPE target:** 8 on sets 2–4.

*The rotation recruits all three deltoid heads plus rotator cuff — more complete shoulder stimulus than a standard press.*

## 3. Accessory B — Lateral Shoulder Raise (Dumbbell)
**3 sets · 90 sec rest between sets**

Your PR is 10 lb per hand.

| Set | Reps | Weight (per hand) |
|-----|------|-------------------|
| 1 | 8 | 10 lb |
| 2 | 8 | 10 lb |
| 3 | 8 | 10 lb |

**Cues:** Slight forward lean, lead with elbows not hands, stop at shoulder height — no higher. Slow 3 sec eccentric.

*Staying at 8 reps here — lateral raises are an isolation movement where mind-muscle connection matters more than load. Add 2 lb per hand in Week 3 if all 3 sets feel RPE 6 or below.*
