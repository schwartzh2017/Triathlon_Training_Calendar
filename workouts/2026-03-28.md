## The Ride

**Goal:** Aerobic endurance base.

This is your longest effort of the week and the most important session for triathlon fitness.

Long hilly bikes build:

- Cardiac stroke volume (the heart gets bigger and stronger)
- Mitochondrial density in the quads and glutes
- Fat oxidation at sub-threshold effort
- Tibial loading tolerance through vibration and sustained muscle contraction

**Effort:** Steady aerobic.

The hills will push you into higher zones briefly — that's fine and expected.

Spend as much time as possible in the **Recovery and Aerobic Endurance zones** (HR < 156 bpm on the flat and downhill sections).

Let the hills take you into Aerobic Power or Threshold briefly.

Don't try to hold back on climbs — just recover on the descents.

## Auto-regulation rule

If legs feel genuinely heavy from Friday's deadlift session: reduce the ride by 20–30min and keep effort very easy.

If legs feel fresh: push toward the longer end of the time range.

Do not white-knuckle a long ride on dead legs — that accumulates fatigue without fitness benefit.
