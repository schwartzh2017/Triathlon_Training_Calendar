## Why tempo intervals start this week

Base phase built your aerobic engine — you can run for 40+ minutes at controlled HR.

Race Prep builds the engine's ceiling — teaching your body to sustain effort at or near threshold.

A sprint triathlon 5km is run at approximately 88–92% max HR. That's threshold territory.

Today is the first step toward being able to sustain that effort off a bike on race day.

**Option B protocol:** 2×5 minutes at comfortably hard effort with 3 minutes easy between. This is threshold-adjacent — harder than easy, not yet full race effort. It's the right entry point given the achilles is still being managed.

## The Run

**Total structure: ~35–40 minutes**

**Warm-up — 10 minutes easy**

Nimbus 26 or Endorphin Speed — your call.

HR ceiling 148 bpm.

Genuinely easy — this protects the quality of the intervals.

**Change to Endorphin Speeds here if you started in Nimbus.**

---

**Interval 1 — 5 minutes at comfortably hard effort**

Target HR: 150–158 bpm.

Target pace: approximately 8:15–8:45/mi — but go by HR, not pace.

This should feel like you could sustain it for 10 minutes but not 20.

Breathing elevated, can say a few words but not hold a conversation.

---

**Recovery — 3 minutes easy**

Drop back to easy jog, HR back below 145 bpm.

Don't walk — keep moving.

---

**Interval 2 — 5 minutes at comfortably hard effort**

Same target: HR 150–158 bpm.

Expect this to feel harder than interval 1 at the same pace — that's normal.

If HR climbs above 160 bpm: back off pace slightly rather than pushing through.

---

**Easy running — 10 minutes**

HR back to easy aerobic range (138–150 bpm).

Shake out the legs, let HR settle.

---

**2 strides**

*Reduced from 4 to 2 — legs have already done quality work today.*

20s each at 85% effort, 40s walk between.

---

**Cool-down walk — 2–3 minutes**

---

## Achilles protocol — mandatory post-run

Straight-leg calf stretch: 30s each side × 2 rounds.

Bent-knee calf stretch: 30s each side × 2 rounds.

---

## Auto-regulation

If achilles tightens during either interval: finish the interval at reduced pace, skip the second interval, do the strides only.

If achilles tightens during warm-up: skip intervals entirely, run easy 25min + 4 strides only.

The intervals are the goal but the achilles is the constraint.

---

## Contrast Therapy

Post-bike as usual.

Cold first — reduces acute threshold effort inflammation.

---

## What to report back

HR during each interval — did you hit 150–158?

Pace during each interval.

How the effort felt — RPE for the interval portion specifically.

Achilles status throughout.

At what point did the run start feeling good vs feeling hard.
