## Context — Week 2 Brick Progression

Last week: 24:32 run off the bike at 8:39/mi — achilles acted up at mile 2, pace dropped.

This week: same moderate bike effort, **28–30 minute run** — the progression is duration, not pace.

The goal is to extend the time on feet off the bike, not to run faster.

Nimbus 26 for the run — higher heel drop protects the achilles.

If achilles tightens before 28 minutes: slow pace significantly rather than stopping. Shuffling forward is better than stopping. Stop only if pain is sharp or swelling begins.

## Run — Off the Bike

**Duration: 28–30 minutes**

**Target HR: 145–158 bpm**

**Shoes: Asics Gel Nimbus 26**

**Expect the first 2–3 minutes to feel heavy** — brick legs pattern. Last week they resolved at 2–3 minutes. Same expected today.

**Pace:** Don't chase last week's 8:39/mi opening pace. Start at 9:15–9:30/mi and let the legs find their rhythm. Controlled is the goal, not fast.

Last week's achilles acted up at mile 2 because the pace was too fast for where the tendon is right now. Nimbus 26 plus controlled pace should manage this better today.

**Mile by mile targets:**

| Mile | Target pace | Target HR |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 9:15–9:45/mi | 148–155 bpm |
| 2 | 9:00–9:30/mi | 150–158 bpm |
| 2.5–3 | Hold pace | Hold HR |
